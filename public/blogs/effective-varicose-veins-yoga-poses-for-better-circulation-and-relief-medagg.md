Varicose veins are common and can cause pain, swelling, and cosmetic concerns. Alongside medical care, yoga can improve circulation, strengthen leg muscles, reduce swelling (via elevation), and lower stress—supporting overall vein health.

## Understanding Varicose Veins

Varicose veins arise when venous valves weaken, leading to blood pooling and enlarged, twisted veins—most often in the legs. Risk factors include age, pregnancy, obesity, genetics, and prolonged sitting/standing.

## How Yoga Helps

- Enhances venous return and systemic circulation
- Strengthens leg and core muscles to support venous flow
- Reduces swelling through leg‑elevating postures
- Lowers stress and balances autonomic tone
- Slow breathing may improve baroreceptor sensitivity and BP control

## Yoga Poses for Varicose Veins

### Legs‑Up‑the‑Wall (Viparita Karani)
Restorative inversion to promote drainage from legs; hold 5–15 minutes with relaxed breathing.

### Supported Bridge (Setu Bandhasana)
Place a block/bolster under sacrum; hold 1–5 minutes to enhance pelvic/leg circulation and reduce pressure.

### Downward‑Facing Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana)
Inverted “V” improves whole‑body circulation and stretches calves/hamstrings; hold 30–60 seconds.

### Boat Pose (Navasana)
Engages core and promotes systemic flow; hold 30–60 seconds with steady breaths.

### Fish Pose (Matsyasana)
Opens chest; improves circulation and supports endocrine balance; hold 15–30 seconds.

### Butterfly (Baddha Konasana)
Improves lower‑body circulation and hip mobility; hold 1–3 minutes.

### Standing Forward Bend (Uttanasana)
Encourages blood return from legs; hold 30–60 seconds with soft knees if tight.

## Extra Practices to Boost Circulation

- Deep diaphragmatic breathing (5–10 minutes daily)
- Gentle stretching for calves/hamstrings/hips
- Mindfulness meditation to reduce stress load

## Lifestyle Support

- Maintain healthy weight; walk/swim regularly
- Use graduated compression stockings during the day
- Elevate legs when resting; avoid prolonged standing/sitting

## Final Thoughts

Yoga complements medical care by improving circulation, reducing swelling, and lowering stress. Severe varicose veins may still need interventional treatment—work with a clinician to tailor your plan while keeping a consistent, mindful yoga practice.
