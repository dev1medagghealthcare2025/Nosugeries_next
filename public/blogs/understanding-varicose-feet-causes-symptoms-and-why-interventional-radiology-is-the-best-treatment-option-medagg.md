Have you felt discomfort, heaviness, or pain in your feet with visible, twisted veins? You may be dealing with **varicose veins on the feet**—common, often overlooked, and potentially symptomatic.

Modern, non‑surgical treatments in **interventional radiology** can resolve them quickly. This article covers causes, symptoms, self‑care, and why image‑guided therapies are first‑line for many.

## What Are Varicose Veins on Feet?

Varicose veins form when venous valves fail, allowing backward flow and pooling. In feet/ankles, veins can become enlarged, gnarled, and bluish‑purple—especially around the arch, instep, or malleoli.

## Causes

### 1. Persistent pressure from body weight
Prolonged loading weakens vein walls/valves—worse with obesity.

### 2. Long periods of standing or sitting
Reduced calf‑pump action → pooling and raised venous pressure.

### 3. Genetic predisposition
Family history increases risk via inherited wall/valve weakness.

### 4. Hormonal changes (e.g., pregnancy)
Higher blood volume and hormones can dilate and weaken veins.

### 5. Obesity
Excess weight raises venous pressure—weight control helps prevention.

## Symptoms

- Prominent, rope‑like swollen veins on the feet
- Dull ache or throbbing, worse after standing/sitting
- Heaviness/fatigue in feet by day’s end
- Swelling in feet/ankles; tightness in shoes
- Skin changes: discoloration, itching, thickening
- Complications if untreated: ulcers, bleeding, or clots (DVT)

## Self‑Care to Reduce Risk

- Elevate feet periodically
- Stay active: walking/calf raises to boost calf pump
- Graduated compression stockings during the day
- Maintain healthy weight
- Avoid prolonged immobility—move regularly

## Why Interventional Radiology Is the Best Treatment Option

IR uses ultrasound guidance to target and treat refluxing veins—minimally invasive, quick recovery, and durable results.

### Benefits

- Non‑surgical: no large incisions/general anesthesia
- Quick recovery: walk in/out; most resume activities in 24–48 hours
- Effective symptom relief and cosmetic improvement
- Favorable safety profile vs open surgery

### Common IR Procedures

1. Endovenous Laser Therapy (EVLT): laser fiber seals the vein with heat
2. Radiofrequency Ablation (RFA): radiofrequency energy closes the vein
3. Foam Sclerotherapy: foam sclerosant collapses the vein under ultrasound
4. Ultrasound‑guided treatments tailored to anatomy

## When to Seek Care

- Severe pain/swelling/redness
- Skin discoloration or ulcers
- Personal/family history of blood clots

## FAQs

### 1. Are varicose veins on the feet dangerous?
They can progress to ulcers or clots if untreated. Monitor changes and seek care for concerning symptoms.

### 2. Can they go away on their own?
No—lifestyle measures help symptoms but don’t eliminate veins. Interventional treatment addresses the cause.

### 3. Recovery time after IR treatments?
Most patients return to routine in 24–48 hours; minor bruising/tenderness resolves quickly.

### 4. Is interventional radiology painful?
Local anesthesia is used; procedures are generally well‑tolerated with minimal discomfort.
