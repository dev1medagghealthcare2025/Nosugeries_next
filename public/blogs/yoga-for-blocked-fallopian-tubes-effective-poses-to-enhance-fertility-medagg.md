## Understanding Blocked Fallopian Tubes

Blocked fallopian tubes are a common cause of infertility, affecting up to 30–40% of women who struggle to conceive. The tubes allow the egg to meet sperm and travel to the uterus. Blockage can be partial or complete; one open tube may still allow natural conception, while bilateral blockage prevents it. Blockages also increase the risk of ectopic (tubal) pregnancy.

## How Can Yoga Help with Blocked Fallopian Tubes?

Yoga may not remove dense scar tissue or adhesions, but it can positively impact reproductive health by:

- Improving blood circulation to reproductive organs
- Reducing pelvic inflammation
- Balancing hormones for ovarian and uterine function
- Relieving stress and anxiety that disrupt hormonal balance
- Supporting detox pathways that promote tissue healing

## Can Yoga Completely Unblock Fallopian Tubes?

Yoga cannot remove firm scars/adhesions or resolve severe infection‑related blockage. It can, however, enhance circulation, reduce inflammation, and optimize hormones—supporting fertility. For complete blockages, medical intervention is required.

## Interventional Radiology – Fallopian Tube Recanalization (FTR)

### What is FTR?

A non‑surgical, image‑guided procedure using a thin catheter to gently open blocked tubes—no incisions.

### Why FTR Matters

- Restores natural fertility by reopening blocked tubes
- Minimally invasive; no general anesthesia
- High technical success (≈80–90% for proximal blocks)
- Preserves tubal function for natural conception

### Best Candidates

- Proximal tubal block (near uterine end)
- Minimal scarring; no severe tubal damage
- Those seeking to conceive naturally without IVF
