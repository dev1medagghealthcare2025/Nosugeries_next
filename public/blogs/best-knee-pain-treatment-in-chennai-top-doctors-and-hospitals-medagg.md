## Understanding Knee Pain

The knee is a complex, load‑bearing joint vulnerable to injuries, mechanical issues, and arthritis. Symptoms include pain, swelling, stiffness, clicking, instability, and reduced mobility. Identifying the root cause is essential for targeted treatment.

### Diagnosing Knee Pain

- Comprehensive history: prior injuries, comorbidities, family history
- Physical exam: swelling, warmth, ROM, strength, stability
- Imaging: X‑ray/MRI/CT to evaluate fractures, meniscal/ligament injury, cartilage wear

---

## Understanding Genicular Artery Embolization (GAE)

A minimally invasive option for chronic knee pain—especially osteoarthritis—performed by interventional radiologists.

### What is GAE?

Inflamed genicular arteries increase pathologic blood flow and pain. GAE reduces this flow, easing inflammation and pain.

### How is GAE done?

1. Evaluation and imaging planning (MRI/CT as needed)
2. Local anesthesia; small wrist/groin access
3. Microcatheter navigated to genicular branches
4. Embolic particles reduce abnormal hyperemia
5. Catheter removal and bandage—procedure ≈ 1–2 hours

### Recovery and Benefits

- Day‑care, minimally invasive
- Quick recovery (days)
- Significant pain relief and improved function
- An alternative to surgery for many patients
