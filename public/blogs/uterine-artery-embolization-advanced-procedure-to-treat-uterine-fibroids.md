# Uterine Artery Embolization: An Advanced Procedure to Treat Uterine Fibroids - medagg

## What are Uterine Fibroids?

![](https://images.surferseo.art/c434a8f3-1af3-4b4b-b2ce-ce0bcc0b5114.jpeg)

Uterine fibroids (myomas) are mostly non‑cancerous growths within the muscular uterine wall. They are common in women of reproductive age and can cause abdominal/pelvic pain, heavy or prolonged menstrual bleeding, pressure symptoms, and sometimes infertility. Untreated, fibroids may complicate future pregnancies in select cases.

Uterine Artery Embolization (UAE), also called Uterine Fibroid Embolization (UFE), is a minimally invasive interventional radiology procedure. Compared with open surgery, UAE typically offers faster recovery, fewer complications, and preserves the uterus, which can be important for women who wish to retain fertility when clinically appropriate.

## Symptoms of Uterine Fibroids

### Heavy bleeding during menstrual cycles

- Prolonged periods, spotting between periods, and anemia from cumulative blood loss are common.

### Menstrual periods lasting more than a week

- Longer duration can contribute to fatigue, pelvic pain, and activity limitations.

### Pelvic pain and pressure

- Fibroid bulk can cause lower abdominal pressure, impacting daily activities and quality of life.

### Urinary symptoms

- Bladder pressure can cause frequency and urgency, and difficulty fully emptying in some cases, increasing UTI risk.

### Constipation and painful intercourse

- Rectal pressure may impair bowel movements; deep dyspareunia can occur depending on fibroid location.

## What causes Uterine Fibroids?

Fibroid development is multifactorial; contributors can include:

- Hormonal influences (estrogen and progesterone)
- Genetics/family history
- Metabolic factors (obesity, dyslipidemia)
- Vitamin D deficiency
- Hypertension and vascular factors
- Alcohol use and other lifestyle contributors

## The IR Way to Treat Fibroids: Uterine Artery Embolization (UAE/UFE)

### What is UAE/UFE?

UAE is a non‑surgical, image‑guided procedure performed by an interventional radiologist. Via a tiny skin puncture at the wrist or groin, a microcatheter is navigated to the uterine arteries supplying the fibroids. Embolic particles are delivered to block abnormal blood flow, starving fibroids of perfusion so they shrink over weeks to months.

#### How it works (overview)

- Local anesthesia and light sedation; no large incisions
- Catheter advanced under X‑ray guidance to uterine arteries
- Tiny particles embolize fibroid‑feeding vessels
- Post‑embolization, fibroids shrink and symptoms typically improve

### Advantages of UAE

- Minimally invasive; no large incisions or sutures
- Uterus‑preserving; avoids hysterectomy in many candidates
- Outpatient or overnight stay; quicker return to routine
- Lower risk of major complications compared to open surgery
- Avoids general anesthesia in most cases

### Recovery and expectations

- Cramping and pelvic discomfort are common for several days; managed with analgesics
- Post‑embolization syndrome (low‑grade fever, malaise, pain) can occur and is treated supportively
- Most resume light activity within a few days and normal routines within 1–2 weeks (varies)
- Symptom improvement often begins within weeks and continues as fibroids shrink

## Potential Risks and Considerations

- Transient amenorrhea or menstrual changes
- Infection (rare)
- Non‑target embolization (very rare with experienced operators)
- Impact on future fertility remains individualized—discuss goals with your clinician
- Pregnancy after UAE is reported but requires case‑by‑case counseling; obstetric risks (e.g., PPH) should be discussed

## Alternatives and Complementary Options

- Myomectomy (surgical removal of fibroids) for uterus preservation in selected cases
- Hysterectomy (definitive removal of uterus) for severe symptoms when childbearing is complete
- Medical therapies (e.g., GnRH analogs, progesterone modulators) for temporary control
- MRI‑guided focused ultrasound (MRgFUS) in selected centers/candidates

## Conclusion

Uterine Artery Embolization is a precise, uterus‑preserving, minimally invasive option for symptomatic fibroids. By embolizing the fibroid blood supply, UAE shrinks fibroids and relieves heavy bleeding, pain, and pressure—often with a quicker recovery than open surgery. Discuss UAE with your healthcare provider to see if you are a candidate and to align treatment with your fertility and quality‑of‑life goals.
