# Operation for Varicose Veins | Treatment & Recovery Guide - medagg

## Understanding Varicose Veins

Varicose veins are swollen, twisted superficial veins—usually in the legs—caused by weak/damaged valves. Risks increase with family history, age, pregnancy, obesity, and prolonged standing/sitting. Beyond cosmetics, they can lead to pain, clots, and ulcers.

## Risks of Untreated Varicose Veins

- Chronic pain and discomfort (heaviness/ache after standing/sitting)
- Swelling and inflammation (ankles/calves)
- Skin changes (discoloration, dryness/itching; eczema or lipodermatosclerosis; venous ulcers near ankles)
- Bleeding from fragile superficial veins
- Blood clots (DVT) and pulmonary embolism risk in severe cases

![](https://images.surferseo.art/8caeebd9-c18d-4fde-a2e8-b396d798ea7b.jpeg)

## Diagnosing Varicose Veins

- Physical exam for swelling, tenderness, and skin changes
- Venous Doppler ultrasound to assess flow, valve function, and clots

## When Is Surgery Necessary?

- Significant pain, swelling, or ulceration
- Threat of clotting/complications
- Severe cosmetic impact/quality‑of‑life concerns
- Failure of conservative care (compression, lifestyle changes)

## Open Surgical Options

### Vein Stripping and Ligation
- Ligation (tying off) and stripping of diseased segments through small incisions
- Suited for large varicose veins; general/spinal anesthesia; longer recovery than minimally invasive methods

![](https://www.dravinashkatara.com/images/dr/varicoseveins/treatment/sls/1_SURGICALLIGATIONANDSTRIPPING.jpg)

### Transilluminated Powered Phlebectomy (TIPP)
- Light under skin guides powered removal via small incisions; anesthesia required
- Useful for larger or clustered veins

![](https://ars.els-cdn.com/content/image/3-s2.0-B9780123695154500314-f28-03-9780123695154.jpg)

## Minimally Invasive Treatments

Modern techniques deliver effective results with less pain and faster recovery.

### VenaSeal Adhesive Closure
- Medical adhesive seals the vein; minimal prep and recovery; often no anesthesia/compression

![](https://frpsclinics.org/wp-content/uploads/VenaSeal-First-Aliquot-Placement-1024x380.jpg)

### Phlebectomy (Ambulatory)
- Removes surface veins via tiny nicks; manual, not powered

![](https://novusspinecenter.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/phlebectomy_1200x752.jpg)

### Sclerotherapy
- Chemical sclerosant collapses small varicose/spider veins; usually no anesthesia

![](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/627967eefc671069b0b94d33/6716260203d078778167af4f_6716247389a7a15aa5db8ae4_Sclerotherapy%2520for%2520Varicose%2520Veins.jpeg)

### Ultrasound‑Guided Foam Sclerotherapy
- Foam sclerosant for medium/large/tortuous veins under ultrasound

![](https://provascularmd.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/12/varithena-procedure-drawing.png)

### Endovenous Laser Ablation (EVLA)
- Laser fiber closes the vein with heat; local anesthesia; high success; quick recovery

![](https://austinvaricosevein.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/shutterstock_1721338162-1.jpg)

### Radiofrequency Ablation (RFA)
- Heat via radiofrequency energy seals the vein; efficient with rapid recovery

![](https://cedarsheart.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/10/endovenous-radio-frequency-ablation.jpg)

## Preparing (Compression and Consent)

- Review and sign informed consent (risks/benefits)
- Stop smoking and avoid certain medicines before procedures as advised
- Compression stockings guidance may be provided pre/post intervention

## Postoperative Care

- Often home same day; have support for 24 hours
- Rest after anesthesia; wear daytime compression for about 12 days

## Recovery and Follow‑Up

- Minor bleeding can occur for 24–48 hours; bruising 3–4 weeks
- Small cuts fade over 9–12 months
- Walk normally to maintain deep venous flow and reduce DVT risk

## Lifestyle Modifications

- Wear compression to reduce swelling
- Exercise regularly (walk, swim, cycle)
- Maintain healthy weight
- Elevate legs and take breaks from prolonged standing/sitting

## Limiting Disease Impact

- Home measures for symptoms and appearance (compression, elevation, exercise)
- Consider minimally invasive procedures to close/remove refluxing veins when indicated

## Conclusion

Effective varicose vein care blends the right procedure with good recovery habits. Whether minimally invasive ablation or traditional surgery is chosen, adherence to aftercare drives outcomes.
