## Let us know what are Varicose Veins :

Varicose veins are swollen, twisted veins that can be seen just under the skin, usually in the legs. The are causes because of weak and damaged valves in the veins. These veins can cause pain, blood clots, or skin ulcers. If not treated properly, they an cause serious issues.

In cases when complications become sever, procedures like Stripping of Varicose Veins will be necessary to do. In this article, we will examine all you should understand about vein stripping: the pros and cons, its operation and the recovery process.

## Varicose Vein Stripping

![](https://www.dravinashkatara.com/images/dr/varicoseveins/treatment/sls/1_SURGICALLIGATIONANDSTRIPPING.jpg)

Vein stripping has been used for decades as a standard treatment for varicose veins. Before the advent of modern, minimally invasive treatments like laser and radiofrequency ablation, it was the go-to method for addressing severe cases.

Vein stripping is a **surgical procedure** used to remove damaged or dysfunctional veins from the legs.

These veins, usually the **Great Saphenous Vein (GSV)** or **Small Saphenous Vein (SSV)**, are no longer functioning properly due to damaged valves. The femoral vein plays a crucial role in this procedure as it is a key component of the axial veins in the lower extremities, and understanding its anatomy is essential for successful treatment.

![](https://www.stridecare.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/01/leg-pain-illustration_venous-system_varicose-veins-blood-clot-risk-2-1024x775.jpg)

Vein stripping helps to eliminate the symptoms and complications associated with these veins and improves overall circulation.

The procedure can be used to prevent venous skin ulcers from returning after treatment. Over time, the technique has been refined to minimize risks and improve outcomes.

## Key Differences : Vein Ligation and Vein Stripping

- Vein ligation ties off a damaged vein so blood stops entering it. The vein remains in the body.
- Vein stripping removes the damaged vein entirely. Ligation is often part of the stripping process.

| Aspect | Vein Ligation | Vein Stripping |
|---|---|---|
| Procedure | Tie off the vein | Remove the vein |
| Extent | Less invasive | More invasive |
| Recovery | 1–2 weeks | 2–4 weeks |
| Suitability | Milder cases or adjunct | Severe/extensive disease |
| Incisions | 1–2 small | Multiple along the vein |
| Effectiveness | Prevents reflux but vein stays | Eliminates vein |

## Symptoms to Seek Medical Attention

- Severe leg pain or swelling
- Open sores/ulcers
- Blood clots or bleeding from veins
- Difficulty walking/standing due to symptoms
- Skin color/texture changes

## When should you consider this procedure?

Typically reserved for complex, severe disease not suitable for minimally invasive options.

- Large varicose veins causing significant discomfort
- Pain, swelling, heaviness, skin irritation
- Venous ulcers or bleeding
- Recurrence after prior treatments

## Benefits of Vein Stripping

- Reduces pain, swelling, and heaviness
- Redirects blood to healthy veins; improves circulation
- Removes visible bulging veins
- Lowers risk of ulcers, bleeding, DVT
- Durable solution for severe cases

## Preparing for Surgery

- Follow pre-op instructions (fasting/medication adjustments)
- Arrange a ride home

## The Surgical Process: Step-by-Step

The goal is to remove or close the refluxing axial vein and reduce symptoms.

### Preparation

- Duplex ultrasound mapping
- Local or general anesthesia

### Surgical Steps

- Small groin incision to access target vein
- Ligation at junction with deep system
- Second incision near knee/ankle; stripper device removes the vein

### After Surgery

- Close incisions; compression bandage applied
- Early walking encouraged; avoid strenuous activity for 1–2 weeks
- Expect bruising, swelling, mild pain for days–weeks
- Compression stockings for 1–2 weeks; attend follow-ups

## Managing Varicose Veins: Preventing Worsening

- Wear graduated compression stockings
- Elevate legs regularly
- Avoid prolonged sitting/standing; move often
- Exercise (calf-pump activities)
- Maintain healthy weight
- Avoid crossing legs/ankles

## Alternatives to Stripping and Ligation

Endovenous thermal ablation treats the vein from inside with heat to stop reflux.

### Radiofrequency Ablation (RFA): A Minimally Invasive Option

A thin catheter delivers radiofrequency heat to close the vein under ultrasound guidance. Over time the vein is resorbed and blood reroutes to healthy channels. Done with local anesthesia, with minimal discomfort and quick recovery, often preferred over surgery.

![](https://cedarsheart.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/10/endovenous-radio-frequency-ablation.jpg)

Benefits:

- Minimally invasive (tiny puncture)
- Quick recovery (light activity in 1–2 days)
- High success (>90%)
- Cosmetic improvement (bulges flatten)
- Lower complication risk
- Outpatient; no general anesthesia
- Long-term relief and low recurrence

## Myths & Facts

1. Removing a vein harms circulation — False. Blood reroutes to healthy veins and overall flow improves.
2. Recovery takes months — False. Most recover in 2–4 weeks (often sooner with minimally invasive options).
3. It’s the only option — False. Endovenous RFA or laser ablation are modern, less invasive alternatives.
