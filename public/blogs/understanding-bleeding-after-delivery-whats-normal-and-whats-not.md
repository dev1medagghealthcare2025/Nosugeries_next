Bleeding After Delivery: What’s Normal, What’s Not, and When to Seek Treatment
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Bleeding after delivery is a natural part of recovery for every new mother. Whether you’ve had a vaginal delivery or a C-section, your body sheds the remaining blood, tissue, and amniotic fluid through vaginal bleeding, commonly known as lochia.

## What Is Normal Postpartum Bleeding?

After giving birth, your body begins to shed the uterine lining that supported your pregnancy. This bleeding is known as lochia and typically follows a predictable pattern:

- Lochia Stage: Lochia rubra
  - Appearance: Bright to dark red blood with occasional large blood clots
  - Duration (approx.): First week after birth
  - Flow characteristics: Heavy bleeding initially, tapering over days
- Lochia Stage: Lochia serosa
  - Appearance: Pinkish or brownish
  - Duration (approx.): 1 to 2 weeks after delivery
  - Flow characteristics: Moderate flow, occasional spotting
- Lochia Stage: Lochia alba
  - Appearance: Yellowish white discharge
  - Duration (approx.): 3 to 6 weeks after delivery
  - Flow characteristics: Light flow or spotting

### How Many Days Do You Bleed After Delivery?

- Normal vaginal delivery: Bleeding may last 4 to 6 weeks, gradually decreasing over time.
- C-section: Slightly less bleeding, but similar duration.

### How Many Days Bleeding After Normal Delivery?

In a normal vaginal delivery, expect:

- Heavy bleeding (lochia rubra) for the first week
- Lighter bleeding and spotting (lochia serosa and lochia alba) for the next few weeks

## When Is Postpartum Bleeding a Concern?

While bleeding after birth is expected, you should call your doctor immediately if you notice any of the following:

- Very large clots (bigger than a golf ball)
- Soaking through a sanitary pad in less than an hour
- Persistent bright red bleeding beyond the first week
- Sudden return of heavy bleeding after it had slowed
- Clammy skin, dizziness, blurred vision, or rapid heartbeat
- A severe drop in blood pressure or signs of shock

These could signal postpartum hemorrhage, a serious complication.

## Understanding Postpartum Hemorrhage

Postpartum hemorrhage (PPH) refers to excessive blood loss after giving birth. It can occur:

- Immediately after delivery
- Within the first 24 hours
- Weeks after delivery (known as secondary PPH)

### How Much Blood Is Too Much?

- Losing up to 500 ml of blood after a vaginal delivery is considered normal.
- Loss of more than 500 ml (or 1000 ml after a C-section) is classified as postpartum hemorrhage diagnosed.

### When Does Postpartum Hemorrhage Occur?

|Timing of PPH       |Typical Onset                  |
|--------------------|-------------------------------|
|Primary PPH         |First 24 hours after birth     |
|Secondary (late) PPH|24 hours to 12 weeks postpartum|

### Causes of Postpartum Hemorrhage

- Cause: Uterine atony (most common)
  - Description: The uterus fails to contract, leading to bleeding vessels
- Cause: Retained placenta
  - Description: Parts of the placenta stay attached, preventing the uterus from shrinking
- Cause: Uterine rupture
  - Description: Uterus tears, often in women with prior C-sections or scars
- Cause: Bleeding disorders
  - Description: Problems with blood clotting
- Cause: Trauma
  - Description: Injury to blood vessels or birth canal

Women with overdistended uterus, large baby, high blood pressure, or multiple pregnancies are at higher risk.

## How Long Can Postpartum Bleeding Last?

Postpartum bleeding duration varies:

- For most women, bleeding after normal delivery lasts about 4 to 6 weeks.
- If you’re still experiencing bleeding after delivery 2 months, it is not typical and should be evaluated. Possible causes include:
  - Retained placenta fragments
  - Uterine atony
  - Postpartum infection
  - Postpartum hemorrhage

In such cases, an advanced treatment option like Interventional Radiology can be highly effective.

## How to Stop Postpartum Bleeding Faster

While you can’t fully stop bleeding immediately (your body needs to shed extra blood and tissue), here are some ways to encourage healthy recovery and prevent postpartum hemorrhage:

|Tip                     |Benefit                                        |
|------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
|Uterine massage         |Helps the uterus contract and reduce blood loss|
|Frequent breastfeeding  |Stimulates uterine contractions via oxytocin   |
|Avoid overexertion      |Allows body to heal, prevents heavy bleeding   |
|Follow hygiene practices|Prevent infection, which can worsen bleeding   |
|Monitor for symptoms    |Early detection of postpartum hemorrhage       |

Important: If you notice abnormal symptoms, call your doctor immediately.

## How Is Postpartum Hemorrhage Treated?

Initial steps for postpartum hemorrhage treated include:

- Uterine massage to stimulate contractions
- Medications to promote uterus contract
- Manual removal if placenta stay attached
- Blood transfusion if there is significant blood loss
- Lab tests to check red blood cells, clotting ability

If these measures fail or bleeding persists, Interventional Radiology offers a non-surgical solution.

## Interventional Radiology: A Non-Surgical Treatment for Postpartum Hemorrhage

### What is Interventional Radiology?

Interventional Radiology (IR) uses minimally invasive image-guided procedures to treat medical conditions, including postpartum hemorrhage, without surgery.

### How Does It Help With Postpartum Bleeding?

The procedure, called Uterine Artery Embolisation (UAE), is highly effective in controlling bleeding after delivery.

- Procedure Step: A small catheter is inserted through the groin
  - Outcome: Minimally invasive, no surgery
- Procedure Step: Guided to the uterine arteries supplying bleeding vessels
  - Outcome: Targeted treatment
- Procedure Step: Tiny particles are injected to block blood flow to the bleeding site
  - Outcome: Stops bleeding while preserving uterine function
- Procedure Step: Blood collects in treated area but safely absorbed later
  - Outcome: Controlled healing

### Advantages of Uterine Artery Embolisation

- No need for surgery
- Short recovery time
- Preserves fertility in most cases
- High success rate in stopping heavy bleeding and avoiding hysterectomy

Women develop a full recovery typically within a few weeks after the procedure.

## Who Should Consider Uterine Artery Embolisation?

UAE is especially useful for women who:

- Have bleeding after delivery 2 months
- Have had postpartum hemorrhage treated unsuccessfully with conventional methods
- Have placenta was attached abnormally (placenta accreta)
- Have medical conditions making surgery risky
- Want to avoid hysterectomy to preserve fertility

It is particularly beneficial for Asian and Hispanic women, who statistically face a higher risk of postpartum hemorrhage.
