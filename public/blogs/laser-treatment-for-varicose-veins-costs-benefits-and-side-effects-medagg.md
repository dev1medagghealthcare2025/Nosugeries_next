# Laser Treatment for Varicose Veins: Costs, Benefits, and Side Effects - medagg

## What is Varicose Veins?

Varicose veins are leg veins that are overfilled or clogged with blood, leading to pooling and increased venous pressure. Over time, this enlarges and twists the veins, impairs circulation, and can progress to complications if untreated. Symptoms often worsen with prolonged standing/sitting.

**Endovenous Laser Ablation (EVLA/EVLT)** is an advanced Interventional Radiology (IR) technique—minimally invasive and highly effective for symptomatic varicose veins, including many larger veins. Patients typically report minimal scarring, faster recovery, and durable relief compared with surgery.

## Symptoms of Varicose Veins

### Visible Veins in the Legs
Prominent, bulging, bluish/purple, and twisted veins, often on one or both legs. Beyond cosmetic concern, untreated disease may progress to complications (e.g., superficial thrombophlebitis or deep vein thrombosis risk in certain settings).

### Pain and Discomfort

![](https://images.surferseo.art/fed4f36f-0505-431a-aef9-6fa44870f03c.jpeg)

Aching, heaviness, cramps, and pain that can worsen with activity or at day’s end. Quality of life can be significantly impacted.

### Skin Changes
Skin discoloration, dryness/itching, or eczema‑like irritation may develop. Advanced disease can lead to indurated skin and venous ulcers.

### Swelling
Ankle/leg swelling reflects venous hypertension. Chronic venous insufficiency can cause cramps, skin changes, and ulcer risk.

## Disadvantages of Traditional Varicose Vein Surgery

### Vein Ligation & Stripping
Surgical ligation (tying off) and stripping (removal) require anesthesia and incisions. Risks include pain, infection, bleeding, nerve injury, and longer recovery. Recurrence can still occur if refluxing sources persist.

## Laser Treatment for Varicose Veins

### Endovenous Laser Treatment (EVLA/EVLT)

![](https://images.surferseo.art/9ffd9c6d-29ac-4f46-baaf-e3a9c932e318.jpeg)

EVLA is a non‑surgical, image‑guided procedure. A thin laser fiber is inserted via a small puncture; laser energy heats the vein wall, causing it to collapse and seal (fibrosis). Blood is then rerouted to healthy veins, improving overall circulation.

EVLA demonstrates high success rates with fewer systemic side effects than open surgery. Many series report >90–95% occlusion rates at follow‑up when performed by experienced IR teams.

## Added Benefits with Endovenous Laser Therapy

### Benefits of Laser Treatment

#### Minimally Invasive
Tiny puncture access—no large incisions, stitches, or sutures. Ultrasound guidance improves precision and outcomes; scarring is minimal and recovery is faster.

#### Outpatient Procedure
Most cases are day‑care with local anesthesia and tumescent infiltration—patients generally go home the same day and resume normal activity in days.

#### Fewer Complications
Compared to open surgery, EVLA has lower risks of bleeding, wound infection, and nerve injury. Overall systemic stress is reduced.

#### Faster Recovery
As a minimally invasive IR technique, EVLA supports rapid return to work and exercise (walking, cycling, gym) with clinician guidance.

#### Improved Appearance
By closing refluxing veins, EVLA reduces bulging and discoloration, with minimal scarring and strong cosmetic satisfaction.

## Cost of Endovenous Laser Treatment

Costs vary by city, facility, and disease extent. As a broad estimate in India, bilateral treatment can be upward of ~₹1.5 lakh, depending on vein segments treated, consumables, and adjunct procedures (e.g., phlebectomy/sclerotherapy). Obtain a personalized quote after ultrasound mapping.

## Potential Side Effects of Laser Treatment for Varicose Veins

While generally safe, EVLA may cause temporary effects:

- Local tenderness, bruising, tightness along the treated vein
- Skin discoloration; rarely, superficial burns
- Transient numbness from tumescent anesthesia
- Rare complications: superficial thrombophlebitis, DVT, infection

Choosing a certified **Interventional Radiologist** with ultrasound‑guided expertise minimizes risks and optimizes results.
