## Exploring Non‑Surgical Approaches: Alternatives to Surgery

Non‑surgical treatments focus on relieving pain, improving function, and enhancing mobility without invasive procedures.

### Genicular Artery Embolization (GAE) — Medagg Healthcare

GAE is a minimally invasive day‑care procedure for knee OA. By reducing abnormal inflammatory blood flow to the synovium, it lowers pain and improves function.

![](https://medagghealthcare.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/Instagram-post-172-300x300.webp)

### Physical Therapy

PT strengthens supporting muscles, improves mobility, and reduces discomfort from injuries, ligament damage, or OA. A therapist assesses ROM, stability, and strength, then tailors exercises.

![Physical Therapy for Knee Pain](https://medagghealthcare.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/Instagram-post-175-300x300.jpg)

### Bracing and Knee Supports

Braces add support and stability—useful for ligament injuries or post‑arthroscopy.

### Medications and Pain Relievers

NSAIDs, topical agents, and in some cases stronger analgesics may be used. Injections (corticosteroid; hyaluronic acid) can offer short‑term relief in selected cases.

## Rehabilitation and Recovery

Whether non‑surgical or surgical, rehab is key. Post‑procedure/surgery physiotherapy rebuilds strength and restores normal function with condition‑specific protocols.

## Conclusion

For effective knee pain treatment in Chennai, consult experienced clinicians who offer personalized care.
