## Understanding Prostate Enlargement

### What is Prostate Enlargement?

Benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH) is common with aging. The prostate (a walnut‑sized gland below the bladder) helps produce seminal fluid. As it enlarges, it can compress the urethra and cause urinary symptoms.

### Symptoms and Risks of Enlarged Prostate

- Frequent urination, especially at night (nocturia)
- Hesitancy and difficulty starting/stopping
- Weak or interrupted stream
- Incomplete emptying; straining
- Painful urination; hematuria
- Pelvic/lower back pain; painful ejaculation
- Erectile dysfunction in some cases

Untreated BPH can lead to UTIs, bladder stones, kidney damage, and acute urinary retention—early evaluation is important.

## Prostatic Artery Embolization (PAE) – A Modern Solution for BPH

PAE is a minimally invasive, image‑guided treatment that reduces prostate size and urinary symptoms with fewer complications and faster recovery than many surgical options.

### How Prostatic Artery Embolization is Done

1. Pre‑procedure evaluation (PSA, imaging) and local anesthesia
2. Small wrist/groin access to radial/femoral artery
3. Microcatheter navigated to prostatic arteries under imaging
4. Embolic particles injected to reduce targeted blood flow
5. Catheter removal and bandage; most patients go home same day or next

### Benefits of PAE

- Minimally invasive; no incisions into the prostate
- Quick recovery with minimal discomfort
- Significant improvements in frequency, flow, and emptying
- Lower risk of sexual side effects compared with some surgeries
